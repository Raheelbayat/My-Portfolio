Who is Raheel Bayat?

Raheel Bayat is a medical student at Tehran University of Medical Sciences (TUMS), located in Tehran, Iran. She was born in Kabul, Afghanistan in 1999, to Mohammad Jaweed Bayat and Munira Bayat. Raheel has three sisters and one brother: Dr. Aqdas Bayat, Mohammad Azim Bayat, Author Zahra Bayat, and Hajar Bayat. Raheel began her primary education in 2007 (1385) and graduated from high school in 2018 (1397). Following her graduation, she immediately pursued a Doctor of Medicine (MD) degree at Khatam Al-Nabieen University in 2019 (1398). From the beginning, she was deeply passionate about medicine and committed herself to achieving her goals. However, after the return of the Taliban regime, Raheel-along with countless other Afghan girls-was forcibly banned from continuing her education. Like millions of girls across the country, she was confined to her home, denied her basic rights, and faced immense pressure from cultural and political oppression, including the threat of early marriage. While many young women lost hope-some even resorting to suicide-Raheel refused to surrender. Instead, she entered the world of research, applied for scholarships, and tirelessly pursued new opportunities. Among her many efforts, she was accepted at TUMS to study medicine once again-proving that destiny, perseverance, and faith can overcome even the harshest obstacles. Raheel bravely challenged deeply rooted societal beliefs, ultimately convincing her family and community to support her dream of continuing her education. Today, she is not only a dedicated MD student, but also an active researcher, web designer, and a speaker for USERN Mtalks. Although she faces the emotional and practical challenges of living alone in a foreign country, she remains committed to improving her skills, fulfilling the goals she has set for herself, and walking the path that Allah (S.W.T.) has written in her destiny.
